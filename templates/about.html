About Nasheman

Our History

Since 1989, Nasheman has been a beacon of hope for differently-abled individuals.

July 1989

Founded under the Department of Social Welfare & Bait-ul-Maal, Punjab, to provide vocational training and rehabilitation.

Over the Years

Trained 2,500–3,000 individuals, empowering them to support themselves and their families with dignity.

Today

Continues to instill courage, confidence, and purpose, helping trainees become respectable citizens.

Changing Perspectives

Transforming societal views on disability.

"Disability is not a weakness but a divine condition."

At Nasheman, we challenge the notion that disability equals helplessness. Our trainees are inspired to perform exemplary services and earn high regard in society, supported by dedicated officers, staff, and social workers of the Social Welfare Department.

Our Objectives

Committed to empowering persons with disabilities.

Public Awareness

Promote the value of persons with disabilities as integral members of society, deserving dignity and respect.

Parental Education

Educate parents to treat their disabled children equally, fostering opportunities for growth and independence.

Employment Awareness

Spread awareness of employment opportunities for the disabled to encourage self-reliance.
